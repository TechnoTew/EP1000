Difference between Raster Graphics and Vector Graphics

Raster graphics are made up of coloured pixels on the screen. This means that pictures, which are a type of raster graphics, are dependent on resolution and cannot be scaled up without a difference in quality. Vector graphics on the other hand are rendered using mathematical formulas. This allows us to scale them up infinitely without a loss in quality. Examples of vector graphics includes fonts.
