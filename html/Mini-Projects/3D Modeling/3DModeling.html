Knight Chess Piece
Requirements
  • Base: 30mm
  • Height: 50mm
  • Head Thickness: 5mm
  • Hollow Base

Step 1: Find a picture to serve as the base of the sketch

Source of picture. I first created a bounding box of 50mm height and 30mm width using a rectangle and scaled the image to fit snugly inside the box. This achieves the base and height requirement of this task

Step 2: Designing the chesspiece bottom

I used the line, fillet, and 3 point arc tools to sketch out a 2d profile of the base of the chestpiece. And afterwards, I used the revolve tool to repeat the plane across the y axis to form the base of the chess piece.

Step 3: Designing the top portion of the chesspiece

For the head of the chess piece, I used the Fit Point Spline, 3 point arc, and lines to design it, and extruded the head of the chess piece to 2.5mm on each side, 5mm total.

Step 4: Printing the chesspiece and clearing supports from print

After printing the chesspiece, I had to remove the supports using pliers and sand the rough edges down

Laser-Cut Box with Lid
Requirements
  • Box must be parametric (changing parameters affects the box)
  • Have a lid

Reference Image for the box (taken from presentation slide)
Box Parameters
Front Face of box

1. A rectangle sketch was created for the front face. The tab spacing for both the side and the front face was also drawn on the sketch. The face is constrained by the length and height defined by the parameters at the start.

2. The tabs along the height of the box were cut out using the extrude tool. This cut is considered a feature, which allows me to use the Rectangular Pattern tool to duplicate the cuts along the height and also mirror it to the other side of the front face.

3. The front tabs along the bottom of the box were also cut out using the extrude tool. And similarly patterned using the tool mentioned in the above step.

Side Face of box

1. We start by creating a new component called side. Similarly to step 1 of the front face, a rectangular sketch was created as the side face. The face is constrained by the height and the width of the box.

2. The tabs for the side face were then created by combining the front face and the side face together, and then using the cut selection, while selecting keep tools. This results in the side face getting cut, while also keeping the front face.

3. The tabs along the bottom of the side face were cut using the extrude tool simlar to step 3 of the front face section, and patterned accordingly.

Back Face of box

1. We start by creating a midplane in between the front and the back faces of the side component.

2. We then mirror the front component using the mirror tool under the creation section, using the midplane as the mirror plane. As we can see, the resultant component is identical to the front component, and also in the right place.

Side Face of box (2)

1. The side section from the side was then combined with the mirrored front face to complete both sides of the tabs of the side face.

2. A midplane was again created, using the leftmost and rightmost faces of the front face

3. The side face was mirrored using the mirror tool across the midplane constructed in the previous step

Base of Box

1. First, the base sketch was created with one of the edges relative to the top face of the bottom tabs of the box. The base was then extruded to the thickness of the material.

2. Next the base sketch was combined with the side panels and cut.

Hinge of Box

1. To make the hinge, I decided to sketch a circle on the side of the box for a peg of size thickness. I went back in the history timeline to edit the side sketch for the side panel. After editing and saving the sketch, the model automatically updated and we end up with hinges on both sides of the box.

Lid of Box

1. To make the lid, the geometries of the side panels of the box were projected onto the lid sketch

2. The sketch was then extruded to the thickness of the thickness, and the lid is completed. This marks the end of the box construction.
